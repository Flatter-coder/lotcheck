LotCheck

Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 13, 2026

LotCheck ("we," "us") is an Alberta-based used car marketplace and buyer-first verification platform. This policy explains what information we collect, why, how it's protected, and what happens in the event of a breach. It's written to comply with Alberta's Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA).

What we collect

When you use LotCheck to browse listings, submit a buyer request, or negotiate an offer with a dealer, we may collect:

  • Your name, email address, and phone number
  • Vehicle interest details (make, model, trim, budget, financing preferences)
  • Offer and negotiation history between you and a dealer
  • General usage data (pages viewed, listings searched) to keep price history and market data accurate
What we deliberately don't collect: we do not ask for or store your driver's license number. If a test drive requires license verification, that happens in person at the dealership, the same way it always has — LotCheck only records a simple self-attestation ("I hold a valid driver's license") at request time.

How we use it

  • To connect your request with AMVIC-licensed dealers and relay offers
  • To calculate Deal Score, price history, and applicable rebate estimates
  • To improve the accuracy of our market data over time

We do not sell your personal information to third parties. Dealers only see the details of your specific request needed to make you an offer — not your browsing activity or contact information for any other purpose.

How it's protected

Data is stored with Supabase, with automated daily backups. Access to LotCheck's admin systems is restricted to authorized personnel only. We follow standard practices for securing data in transit (HTTPS) and at rest.

If a breach occurs

Alberta is the only province with a mandatory private-sector breach notification law under PIPA (s. 34.1). If a breach of security safeguards creates a real risk of significant harm to you — such as identity theft, financial loss, or reputational damage — LotCheck will:

  • Notify the Office of the Information and Privacy Commissioner of Alberta (OIPC) without unreasonable delay
  • Notify affected individuals directly, if the OIPC determines that's warranted
  • Keep an internal record of the breach regardless of whether it meets the notification threshold

You can read more about this requirement directly at oipc.ab.ca/breach-notification.

Your rights

Under PIPA, you have the right to access the personal information LotCheck holds about you and to request corrections if it's inaccurate. To make a request, contact us using the details below.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes materially, we'll update the effective date above. Continued use of LotCheck after a change means you accept the updated terms.
